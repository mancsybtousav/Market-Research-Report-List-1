<p><p>Fiber Reinforced Polymer (FRP) rebars are composite materials made from a combination of fibers, such as glass, carbon, or basalt, embedded in a polymer resin matrix. They are used as a substitute for traditional steel reinforcement in concrete structures due to their lightweight, corrosion-resistant, and non-conductive properties.</p><p>The FRP rebars market is experiencing significant growth, driven by the increasing demand for durable and sustainable construction materials. The market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 9.9% during the forecast period. The demand for FRP rebars is particularly high in regions prone to corrosion, such as coastal areas, as well as in seismic zones where the lightweight nature of FRP rebars can improve structural performance.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the FRP rebars market is the development of hybrid rebars that combine different types of fibers to enhance performance and cost-effectiveness. Manufacturers are also focusing on improving the production process and quality control of FRP rebars to meet the stringent requirements of construction projects.</p><p>Overall, the FRP rebars market is poised for continued growth as construction industries worldwide increasingly recognize the benefits of using FRP rebars in infrastructure projects.</p></p>
