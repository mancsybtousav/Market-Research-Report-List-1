<p><p>Activated cake emulsifiers are additives used in cake production to improve texture, increase shelf life, and enhance overall quality. The market types include polyglycerol esters, monoglycerides, lactic acid esters, polysorbates, and others. Polyglycerol esters are commonly used for stabilization, while monoglycerides act as an emulsifier and improve moisture retention. Lactic acid esters help with dough conditioning, while polysorbates improve volume and texture. Other emulsifiers may include sorbitan esters, diacetyl tartaric acid esters, or other specialized blends for specific baking needs.</p></p>
