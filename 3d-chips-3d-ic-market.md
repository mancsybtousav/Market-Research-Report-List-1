<p><p>3D chips, also known as 3D ICs, are a type of integrated circuit where multiple layers of electronic components are vertically stacked to save space and improve performance. The market types for 3D chips include 3D Wafer-Level Chip-Scale Packaging (WLCSP), which involves thinning and stacking individual dies on a wafer level; 3D TSV, which uses through-silicon vias to connect stacked dies vertically; and others, which may include technologies like microbumps or interposers for connecting the layers.</p></p>
