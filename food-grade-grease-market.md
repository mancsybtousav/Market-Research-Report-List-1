<p><strong>The Food Grade Grease Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>H1 Lubricants</li><li>H2 Lubricants</li><li>3H (Releasing Agents)</li><li>H3 (Soluble Oils)</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Food grade grease refers to lubricants and releasing agents that are safe for use in food processing and packaging applications. There are several types of food grade grease available in the market. H1 lubricants are grease formulations that are intended for incidental food contact, meaning that they may come into contact with food but are not intended to be consumed. H2 lubricants, on the other hand, are not intended for any contact with food. Releasing agents, also known as 3H lubricants, are used to prevent food from sticking to processing equipment. Soluble oils, or H3 lubricants, are mixtures of oil and water used in food processing machinery. Additionally, there may be other types of food grade grease available, each with its own specific use or function in the food industry.</p></p>
