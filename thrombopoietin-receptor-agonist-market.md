There are two main types of Thrombopoietin Receptor Agonists on the market: Peptide Agents and Small Molecule Agents. Peptide Agents are larger molecules made up of amino acids, while Small Molecule Agents are smaller, chemically synthesized compounds. Both types of agents work by binding to the Thrombopoietin receptor on platelet-producing cells, signaling them to increase platelet production. These medications are important in treating disorders like immune thrombocytopenia and aplastic anemia.</p></p>
