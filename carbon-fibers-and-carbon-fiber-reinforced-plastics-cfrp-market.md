<p><p>Carbon fibers are composed of carbon atoms bonded together in a crystal alignment. PAN-based carbon fiber is produced from polyacrylonitrile precursors and is the most common type used in industries. Pitch-based carbon fiber is derived from petroleum or coal tar pitch. Carbon fibers based on other precursors involve alternative materials such as rayon or lignin. Carbon fiber reinforced plastics (CFRP) combine these carbon fibers with a polymer matrix to create a strong and lightweight composite material used in aerospace, automotive, and sporting goods industries.</p></p>
