<p><p>Refractory materials are categorized into three main types based on their temperature resistance. Common refractory materials can withstand temperatures ranging from 1580℃ to 1770℃, while advanced refractory materials have a higher tolerance of 1770℃ to 2000℃. Superrefractory materials are the most heat-resistant, capable of withstanding temperatures exceeding 2000℃. Each type serves a specific purpose in industries where high temperatures are present, such as steel and glass manufacturing. These materials play a crucial role in maintaining the integrity of equipment and structures in extreme heat conditions.</p></p>
