<p><p>Nitrilotriacetic Acid (NTA) is produced through three main methods: Chloroacetic Acid Method, Strygger Method, and Carboxymethylated Method. The Chloroacetic Acid Method involves the reaction between chloroacetic acid and ammonia to produce NTA. The Strygger Method uses hydrocyanic acid as a starting material to synthesize NTA. The Carboxymethylated Method involves the reaction between sodium chloroacetate and ethylene diamine to produce NTA. These methods cater to different market needs and applications for Nitrilotriacetic Acid.</p></p>
