<p><p>Gene therapy products can be categorized into two main types: ex vivo and in vivo therapies. Ex vivo therapies involve the extraction and modification of the patient's cells outside the body before reintroducing them back into the patient. In contrast, in vivo therapies involve directly administering the gene therapy product into the patient's body for targeted treatment. Both approaches have shown promise in treating a variety of genetic disorders and diseases, providing potential new options for patients in need of personalized therapies.</p></p>
