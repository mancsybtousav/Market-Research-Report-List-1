<p><p>Industrial diesel engines are commonly categorized into two main types: two-stroke and four-stroke engines. Two-stroke engines complete the combustion process in two piston movements, resulting in a power stroke for every revolution of the crankshaft. On the other hand, four-stroke engines complete the combustion process in four piston movements, with one power stroke for every two revolutions of the crankshaft. Each type has its own advantages and disadvantages, depending on factors such as efficiency, power output, and emissions control requirements.</p></p>
