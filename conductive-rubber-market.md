<p><p>Conductive rubber markets are divided into two main types: Type I and Type II. Type I conductive rubber typically consists of natural rubber or silicone based materials with conductive particles added for enhanced conductivity. This type is commonly used in applications requiring flexibility and durability, such as in electronic devices and wearable technology. On the other hand, Type II conductive rubber is made from synthetic rubber compounds with added conductive fillers, making it suitable for more specialized applications requiring higher resistance to chemicals and harsh environments.</p></p>
