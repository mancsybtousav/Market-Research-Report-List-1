<p><p>Spunmelt nonwovens refer to a type of nonwoven fabric produced by combining spunbond and meltblown technologies. Meltblown nonwoven market involves a process where fibers are melted and blown onto a conveyor belt to form a fine web, producing a lightweight and porous material ideal for filtration applications. On the other hand, spunbond nonwoven market involves spinning continuous filaments of polymers and bonding them together to create a strong and durable fabric used in various industries such as hygiene products and automotive applications.</p></p>
